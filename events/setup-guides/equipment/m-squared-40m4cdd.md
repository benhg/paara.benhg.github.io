## Overview

This page should capture the club-specific assembly, mounting, testing, teardown, and storage steps for the 40M4CDD.

The antenna is large enough that the exact order of assembly matters. When in doubt, keep the boom supported, confirm element orientation before tightening hardware, and verify that all guying, truss, and feedline routing is correct before hoisting.

Use the socket sizes and hand tools that match the current trailer hardware.

## Assembly

1. Lay out the boom sections, elements, clamps, matching hardware, and truss parts in the order they will be used.
2. Verify the boom and element markings before attaching any hardware.
3. Assemble the boom and install the elements, leaving hardware snug until the full layout is confirmed.
4. Attach the matching hardware and feedpoint connections.
5. Install the truss or support system and confirm the boom is level.
6. Tighten all hardware after the antenna is square and correctly oriented.

## Mounting

Mounting on the tower trailer should be coordinated with the trailer captain and done with enough hands to control the boom, feedline, and any rotating hardware.

Before hoisting:

1. Confirm the antenna is fully assembled and checked at ground level.
2. Secure the feedline so it will not twist or snag during lift.
3. Verify the trailer mast or mount is ready to accept the antenna.
4. Hoist slowly and keep the boom supported until the final hardware is locked down.

## Testing

Before the antenna is put on the air:

1. Check continuity and feedpoint connections.
2. Verify the antenna is oriented correctly.
3. Sweep SWR on the intended operating band.
4. Confirm the boom, mast, and feedline are stable under load.

## Teardown

Teardown is the reverse of assembly:

1. Disconnect and secure the feedline.
2. Lower the antenna carefully with the boom supported.
3. Remove any truss or support hardware before separating boom sections or elements.
4. Pack the hardware so the marked parts remain paired with the antenna sections they belong to.
5. Inspect for bent elements, loose clamps, cracked insulators, or damaged feedpoint hardware before storage.

## Common Gotchas and FAQs

- Keep the boom supported any time the antenna is partially assembled.
- Do not rely on memory for element order or orientation. Verify the markings before tightening clamps.
- The 40 m beam is a tower-trailer antenna, so plan for more hands and more time than a smaller yagi.
